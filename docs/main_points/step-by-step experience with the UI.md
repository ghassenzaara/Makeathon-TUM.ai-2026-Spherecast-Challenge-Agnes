### **1. Who is the User?**

Meet **Sarah, the Category Sourcing Manager** at our fictional CPG company, GlobalSnacks.

- **Her Job:** She is responsible for buying all the "sweeteners and acidulants" (sugar, citric acid, syrups) for 15 different factories.
    
- **Her Pain Point:** She is drowning in spreadsheets. She _knows_ she is overpaying because her factories buy from dozens of fragmented suppliers, but she is terrified of consolidating. If she accidentally buys a non-organic sugar for an organic product, a massive recall will happen, and she will be fired.
    
- **Her Mindset:** Highly skeptical, risk-averse, and time-poor. **She does not want a black-box AI.** She wants an AI assistant that does the heavy lifting but lets _her_ make the final, safe decision.
    

---

### **2. The User UI: The "Agnes" Dashboard**

Since UI polish isn't a hackathon priority, keep the design brutally functional and clean. Think of a web app split into three main sections:

#### **A. The "Opportunities" Inbox (The Hook)**

When Sarah logs in, she doesn't see a blank search bar. She sees a prioritized list of proactive recommendations generated by your backend pipeline.

- **Card 1:** 🚨 _High Value:_ Consolidate Citric Acid (Estimated Savings: $23,500/yr).
    
- **Card 2:** ⚠️ _Compliance Risk:_ Supplier Z organic certificate expiring in 30 days. Explore substitutes.
    
- **Card 3:** 💡 _Quick Win:_ Swap Vanilla Extract to Supplier Y (Saves 4 days lead time).
    

#### **B. The Deep Dive View (The AI Reasoning)**

Sarah clicks on the "Consolidate Citric Acid" card. The screen opens into a detailed workspace. This is where you win the hackathon. It shows:

- **The "Before & After" Math:** A simple table showing her 3 current suppliers vs. the 1 proposed supplier, highlighting the volume discount.
    
- **The Substitution Logic:** A visual graph or list showing that "Plant A's E330" and "Plant B's Anhydrous Acid" have been identified as 99% functionally identical.
    

#### **C. The "Trust & Evidence" Panel (The Closer)**

This is the most critical part of the UI. Next to the math, there is a panel dedicated entirely to compliance.

- It lists the strict constraints of the final products (e.g., "Must be Non-GMO", "Must be Kosher").
    
- Next to each constraint is a **Green Checkmark ✅**.
    
- **The Interaction:** When Sarah clicks the checkmark, a modal pops up. It displays the exact text the AI scraped from the internet (e.g., _"Extracted from Supplier Y Technical Sheet, Page 2: Manufactured via non-GMO microbial fermentation"_), complete with a hyperlink to the original source document.
    

---

### **3. The User Journey (Step-by-Step)**

1. **Discovery:** Sarah logs in on a Monday morning and clicks the top recommendation: "Consolidate Citric Acid."
    
2. **Reviewing the Tradeoffs:** She reads Agnes's summary: _"Moving all volume to Supplier Y saves 15%, but increases lead time by 2 days. Supplier Y meets all compliance requirements."_
    
3. **Verification (The "Aha!" Moment):** Sarah is skeptical about the Organic compliance. She clicks the "View Evidence" button next to the Organic requirement. Agnes shows her the exact USDA database entry it scraped confirming Supplier Y is certified.
    
4. **Intervention (Optional):** Let's say Sarah knows Supplier Y has bad customer service (human knowledge the AI doesn't have). She types into a feedback box: _"Reject Supplier Y, find the next best alternative."_ Agnes instantly re-calculates and offers Supplier X.
    
5. **Action:** Satisfied with the evidence, Sarah clicks a big **"Approve & Export to ERP"** button. The system updates the internal database and triggers the emails to the suppliers.
